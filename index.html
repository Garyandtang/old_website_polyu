Hello, I'm Jiawei.

I am a first-year MPhil student at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology working with Prof. Ling Shi. I obtained my bachelor degree at the Hong Kong Polytechnic University. In the summer of 2018, I worked in Robot Perception Lab at Carnegie Mellon University with Prof. Michael Kaess. Prior to that I was a power electronics intern at ASM Pacific Technology.

I love building robots, and enjoy every moment of playing with them. I am experienced in robotic perception, image processing, computer vision, embedded system development and deep learning. Currently, I research on distriubtion optimization. My ultimate goal for research is to explore the intelligent embedded system and computer vision to develop a vision-based robot to improve people's livelihood.

In my undergraduate life at PolyU, I spent a lot of time on service-learning. My experience of service-leraning started from a service-learning course to Cambodia in my freshman year. And in the following 2 years I acted as a student assistant to keep developing the villageS in Cambodia. I was also a team leader of a service-learning project to Ya'an, Sichuan, where we constructed a 3D printing building for post-disaster reconstruction. I am very grateful to Dr. Grace Ngai for taking me to the world of service-learning. She let me find the value as an engineering student and made me carefully consider what I should do to further contribute to our society.

 Low-Resolution Face Recognition

In my final-year project, I solved the low-resolution face recognition problem by considering the difference between the high-resolution features and low-resolution features. Meanwhile, I developed an online face verfication demo page for this FYP based on Flask, HTML, javascript and CSS.
